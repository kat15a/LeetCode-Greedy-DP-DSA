Problem: Min Cost Climbing Stairs

Problem Statement:
You are given an integer array cost where cost[i] is the cost
of the ith step on a staircase.

Once you pay the cost, you can climb either:
- one step
- two steps

You can start from step 0 or step 1.

Task:
Return the minimum cost to reach the top of the floor.

Approach:
- Use recursion with memoization.
- To reach step n, we must come from:
  - step n-1
  - step n-2
- The minimum cost for each step is:
  
  cost[n] + min(dp[n-1], dp[n-2])

- Use a DP array to store previously computed results
  and avoid recomputation.

Algorithm Used:
- Recursion
- Dynamic Programming (Top-Down / Memoization)

Time Complexity:
- O(N)

Space Complexity:
- O(N)
