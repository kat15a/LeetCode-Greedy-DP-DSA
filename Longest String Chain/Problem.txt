Problem: Climbing Stairs

Problem Statement:
You are climbing a staircase with n steps.

Each time you can either climb:
- 1 step
- 2 steps

Task:
Return the number of distinct ways to reach the top.

Approach:

At every step we have two choices:
1. Take one step
2. Take two steps

The number of ways to reach step n is:

ways(n) = ways(n-1) + ways(n-2)

This is because:
- From step (n-1) we can take one step to reach n
- From step (n-2) we can take two steps to reach n

We use recursion with memoization (Top-Down Dynamic Programming)
to store previously computed results and avoid repeated calculations.

Algorithm Used:
- Recursion
- Dynamic Programming (Top-Down / Memoization)

Time Complexity:
O(N)

Space Complexity:
O(N)
